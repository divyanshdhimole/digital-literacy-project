## What Is Phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime in which an attacker impersonates a trusted
organisation — such as a bank, university, or government body — to
trick a victim into revealing sensitive information like passwords,
OTPs, or bank account details. It is one of the most common forms of
cybercrime targeting students in India.

---

## Realistic Case Study: "The Fake Scholarship Email"

Rohan, a 19-year-old first-year B.Tech student in Bhopal, received an
email one morning that appeared to come from his university's
scholarship office. The subject line read:
**"Urgent: Verify Your Details to Receive ₹15,000 Merit Scholarship."**

### How It Happened — Step by Step

1. **Bait sent:** Rohan received a professional-looking email with the
   university logo and a forged sender address
   (scholarships-nit@university-india.net) — slightly different from
   the real domain.

2. **Urgency created:** The email warned that his scholarship would be
   cancelled within 24 hours if he did not verify his bank details
   immediately.

3. **Fake link clicked:** Rohan clicked the link, which led to a
   website that looked exactly like his university's student portal.

4. **Credentials entered:** He entered his student login ID, password,
   and then his UPI ID and bank account number on a "payment
   verification" form.

5. **Data harvested:** Within minutes, the attacker used his UPI ID to
   initiate a fraudulent transaction. Rohan lost ₹8,500 from his
   account before he realised something was wrong.

6. **Discovery:** Rohan only noticed the fraud when he received an SMS
   alert from his bank about an unknown debit.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

- First-year college students unfamiliar with online security
- Students actively waiting for scholarships, internship offers, or
  exam results
- Users who do not verify email sender addresses or website URLs
