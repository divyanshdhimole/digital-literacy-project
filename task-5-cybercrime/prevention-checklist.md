# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

## 🔐 General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Always verify the sender's email address before clicking any link.**
      Phishing emails often use addresses like `support@university-india.net`
      instead of official domains. Hover over links to preview the real URL
      before clicking.

- [ ] **2. Never share your OTP, password, or PIN with anyone — ever.**
      No legitimate bank, university, or government portal will ever ask
      for your OTP over call, SMS, or email. If someone asks, it is a scam.

- [ ] **3. Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
      Combine uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password
      manager (like Bitwarden or Google Password Manager) so you do not
      reuse the same password across platforms.

- [ ] **4. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
      Turn on 2FA for your email, college portal, banking apps, and social
      media. This adds a second layer of protection even if your password
      is stolen.

- [ ] **5. Only use trusted, secured Wi-Fi networks.**
      Avoid logging into banking apps or entering personal details on
      public or unknown Wi-Fi (cafes, buses, railway stations). Use your
      mobile data or a VPN instead.

- [ ] **6. Keep your phone and apps updated.**
      Software updates patch known security vulnerabilities. Enable
      automatic updates for your OS and banking/UPI apps to stay
      protected against the latest threats.

---

## 💸 Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

- [ ] **7. Never scan a QR code sent by a stranger to "receive" money.**
      In UPI, you NEVER need to scan a QR code or enter your PIN to
      receive a payment. Scammers send fake QR codes that actually
      DEBIT your account. Receiving money requires no action from your side.

- [ ] **8. Set a daily UPI transaction limit and enable transaction alerts.**
      In your UPI or banking app settings, cap your daily transfer limit
      to a safe amount (e.g., ₹5,000 for daily use). Enable instant SMS
      and app alerts for every transaction so you spot fraud immediately.

---

## 📱 Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **9. Do not accept friend requests or follow requests from unknown accounts.**
      Fake profiles are used to gather personal data, run romance scams,
      or send phishing links. Always verify before accepting.

- [ ] **10. Never post photos of your documents online.**
       Avoid sharing images of your Aadhaar card, PAN card, marksheets,
       or admit card — even in private groups. These can be used for
       identity theft or loan fraud.

## 🚨 How to Report a Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know has been a victim of cybercrime:

```
🌐 Online Portal : https://cybercrime.gov.in
📞 Helpline Number: 1930  (National Cyber Crime Helpline — 24x7)
🏛️  Local Option  : Visit your nearest Cyber Crime Police Station
```

**Steps to report online:**
1. Go to cybercrime.gov.in
2. Click "Report Cyber Crime"
3. Choose category (Financial Fraud / Social Media / Other)
4. Fill in the incident details and submit
5. Save your complaint acknowledgement number for follow-up

> **Report immediately** — the faster you report financial fraud,
> the higher the chance of transaction reversal.
